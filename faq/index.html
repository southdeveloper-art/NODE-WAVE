INFRASTRUCTURE FAQ

Are your servers resold from another provider?

No. NodeWave operates its own compute infrastructure. Our servers run on privately managed nodes powered by Ryzen 9000 series processors, DDR5 5600MT/s memory, and NVMe storage. Each node is integrated into our cluster architecture and monitored through live telemetry to ensure stable performance.

How are our servers protected from DDoS attacks?

All NodeWave servers are protected by dedicated multi-layer DDoS mitigation. Incoming traffic is filtered through a global mitigation network with 210 Tbps+ filtering capacity before reaching our infrastructure, defending against SYN floods, UDP amplification, and more.

What is the difference between GCS and GDS servers?

GCS (Game Compute Servers) provide shared compute resources optimized for scalable game hosting. GDS (Game Dedicated Servers) provide exclusive compute resources reserved for a single client, delivering maximum performance and isolation for demanding workloads.

Where are your servers located?

Our primary infrastructure is located in Frankfurt, Germany, one of the largest network hubs in Europe. This provides excellent connectivity and low latency across the continent due to its proximity to the major internet exchange operated by DE-CIX.

Can I upgrade my server resources later?

Yes. Our infrastructure is built on clustered compute nodes, allowing resources to be adjusted as your needs grow. You can upgrade CPU allocation, memory, or storage without migrating to a completely new server, helping your community scale smoothly.

What hardware powers your servers?

NodeWave servers run on high-frequency AMD Ryzen 9 9950X3D processors, DDR5 5600MT/s RAM, and enterprise NVMe storage. We use own networking hardware for 17Tbit DDoS mitigation. These components deliver strong single-thread performance and fast storage access needed for stable game server performance.
